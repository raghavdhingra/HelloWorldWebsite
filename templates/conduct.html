Introduction

The Following Code of Conduct is for every core-member of society every new member who joins us in the core team and everyone who work with us by doing projects attending classes or in any other way

Ethical Principles and Core Values
  • 1.) Honesty
  • 2.) Integrity
  • 3.) Trustworthiness
  • 4.) Respect For Others
  • 5.) Responsibility
  • 6.) Accountability
  • 7.) Obedience to the rules
  • 8.) Empathy
  • 9.) TeamWork
  • 10.) Commitment to the code
Decision Making and the Code of Conduct

When making a decision, ask yourself the following:

  • 1.) Is it good for students?
  • 2.) Does it comply with society's laws?
  • 3.) Does it reflect values and ethics?
  • 4.) Does it respect the rights of others?
  • 5.) If you are unsure about any of the answers, ask.

Then ask the group for suggestions and take permission of any Mentor or majority to confirm it

Reporting/Speaking Up

The society encourages all its members to ask questions and raise issues without fear of anything that you feel and it is committed to treating reports seriously and investigating them thoroughly.
Members must report suspected unethical, illegal or suspicious behavior of anyone, including the President immediately. The society does not tolerate retaliation against anyone who makes a good faith report of suspected misconduct or otherwise assists with an investigation or audit.
To report a concern:

  • 1.) Talk to any member
  • 2.) Contact a senior member
  • 3.) Contact the Mentors
  • 4.) Contact the President
  • 5.) Make a confidential and/or anonymous report online on whatsapp
No Retaliation

Members who report a concern in good faith cannot be subjected to any adverse action including:

  • 1.) Unfair dismissal
  • 2.) Unfair denial of a promotion
  • 3.) Bullying and harassment, either in person or online
  • 4.) Exclusionary behavior
  • 5.) Any other behavior that singles out the person unfairly
Equal Opportunity

We will not tolerate discrimination based on race, color, religion, gender, age, national origin, sexual orientation, marital status, disability or any other protected class.

Harassment

Treat all fellow Seniors, Juniors and members with dignity and respect at all times we are a family here.
Any type of harassment, including physical, sexual, verbal or other, is prohibited and can result in disciplinary action up to, and including, termination.
Harassment can include actions, language, written words or objects that create an intimidating or hostile work environment, such as:

  • 1.) Yelling at or humiliating someone
  • 2.) Physical violence or intimidation
  • 3.) Unwanted invitations or comments
  • 4.) Visual displays such as derogatory or sexually-oriented pictures or gestures
  • 5.) Physical conduct including assault or unwanted touching
Bullying

We are committed to ensuring that our core team and our members work in safe and respectful environment that is free of bullying.
Bullying can include:

  • 1.) Spreading malicious rumor or gossip.
  • 2.) Excluding or isolating someone socially
  • 3.) Establishing impossible deliverables
  • 4.) Withholding necessary information or purposefully giving the wrong information
  • 5.) Intimidating someone
  • 6.) Impeding someone’s work
  • 7.) Unfairly denying training, leave or promotion
  • 8.) Constantly changing work guidelines
  • 9.) Sending offensive jokes or messages
  • 10.) Criticizing or belittling someone constantly
  • 11.) Tampering with a person's personal belongings or work equipment
Conflicts of Interest

A conflict of interest can occur when a member’s personal activities, investments or internships or ability to act in the best interests. Members should avoid the types of situations that can give rise to conflicts of interest.
It’s important for members to disclose any relationships with anyone of the opposite or same gender, associations or activities with other societies, clearly we encourage you to be in all the societies if you can manage them, but it is required to tell you are part of which of them so to avoid conflict of interest.

External Communication on Behalf of the Society

Each member has the right to communicate with whomsoever they think they should, but should refer all requests for information or interviews to the Team.
Interviews cannot be conducted by one person the complete team should be present.

Privacy

The society complies with the requirements of the country’s and international privacy laws. All members should agree to an agreement that contains provisions for information confidentiality and non-disclosure
The society and its members do not disclose any private, personal information of:

  • 1.) Members
  • 2.) Students
  • 3.) Events
  • 4.) Funds
  • 4.) Future Plans

We all should store all personal information securely, mark it as confidential and store it only for as long as it is needed for the purpose for which is was collected.
When providing personal information, there should be limit access for the students only those with a clears need for the information should be given by the same person whose information is given.
Example: If contact number of X is given then only X has right to give it
Members are required to report any breaches of privacy, including the loss, theft of or unauthorized access to personal information, to the Society.

Competition, Fair Dealings and Lack of trust

While the societies compete aggressively for new recruits, bringing new skills but reputation is built upon trust so while any speech or talking nothing should be said about them let the student analyze which is better out there.
Members are required to:

  • 1.) Communicate the societies task and services in a manner that is fair and accurate, and that discloses all relevant information
  • 2.) Consult a Core Member before engaging in any new practice
  • 3.) Should never ask for money from students or from the society for what they have done and should not indulge in any other anti-competitive activities
  • 4.) Advise the society immediately of possible violations of fair competition practices by our members or by other societies
Gifts and Entertainment

While gifts and entertainment among can be appropriate ways to strengthen ties and build goodwill, they also have the potential to create the perception that decisions are influenced by them. The society is committed to winning hearts only on the merits of its members, services and events.
Members are to:

  • 1.) Use sound judgment and comply with the law, regarding gifts and other benefits
  • 2.) Never allow gifts, entertainment or other personal benefits to influence decisions or undermine the integrity
  • 3.) Never accept gifts or entertainment that are illegal, immoral or would reflect negatively on the society
  • 4.) Never accept cash, cash equivalents, stocks or other securities
  • 5.) Use sound judgment and comply with the law, regarding gifts and other benefits
  • 6.) Use sound judgment and comply with the law, regarding gifts and other benefits

Members may accept occasional personal gifts of nominal value.
Entertainment is allowed unless it doesn’t hurt sentiments of anyone.
When in doubt, everyone should check with the ethics and compliance officer before giving or receiving anything of value.

Charitable Contributions

The society may make charitable contributions to causes and organizations that are not politically affiliated or to anyone in serious need.
But the final decision to contribute lies in the hands of the society so majority will play a role to decide.

Record Keeping

All documents, databases, voice messages, mobile device messages, computer documents, files and photos are records.
Members are required to:

  • 1.) maintain these records and protect their integrity for as long as required
  • 2.) maintain official record keeping systems to retain and file records required for signatures ,to show to authorities, to prove a point in an argument

The society requires all members to protect yourself.
Other assets include facilities, equipment, funds and goodies.

Health Family and Safety

Your Family is your priority we will be there with you for 4 years only but your family is with you life time.
Health is really important kindly look after that too.
If any work is causing issues with your family not able to give family time and is affecting your health that should be told to any member and should be stopped immediately.
All members are expected to perform their work in compliance with applicable health and safety laws and apply safe work practices at all times in all locations.
Members are required to immediately report injuries, illnesses or unsafe conditions.

Environment

The society is committed to operating in an environmentally responsible manner, from the provision of equipment’s and services to conducting activities.
The society complies with all applicable environmental laws and regulations

Use of Social Media
The society respects the right of members to use social media for personal and professional purposes.
Members are responsible for complying with the policies and procedures when communicating on social media. Members are accountable for any information they publish online.
Share confidential information about the society, its events, resources or future plans.
Post comments or pictures that could harm the reputation or is unethical.
Members are required to:
  • 1.) Not post anything against any community on society pages
  • 2.) Respect others no one to allow to leave groups or to kick someone from groups
  • 3.) Ensure any information posted about the events or future activities on students group is correct and well communicated with the members and proper permissions are taken by the authorities
  • 4.) Comply with the rules of the social media sites they use.
Members must not:
“Pretext”, or pretend to be someone they are not.
Speak on behalf of the society if they are not expressly authorized to do so.
Share confidential information about the society, its events, resources or future plans.
Post comments or pictures that could harm the reputation or is unethical.
